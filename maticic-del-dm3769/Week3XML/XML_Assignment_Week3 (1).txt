<note type="imitation" place="bottom" anchored="false"><bibl>Ov. AA 3.219-20</bibl>
<quote><lb/>quae nunc nomen habent operosi signa Myronis,

<note type="commentary" place="bottom" anchored="false"><bibl>Wheeler (1995: 105)</bibl>
<quote>"Ovid makes a pun on <seg rend="italic">iners</seg>,meaning both sluggish and lacking art, which clarifies the absence of <seg rend="italic">ars</seg>
in <seg rend="italic">rudis indigestaque moles</seg>. The phrase pondus iners itself derives from
Ars Amatoria, where Ovid describes the formless stone that is improved
by the sculptor's art: <bibl>Ov. AA 3.219-20</bibl><quote><seg rend="italic"><lb/>quae nunc nomen habent operosi signa Myronis,
<lb/>pondus iners quondam duraque massa fuit</seg>.</quote> What distinguishes 
chaos from cosmos, then, is the ameliorating influence of art.</quote></note>.
